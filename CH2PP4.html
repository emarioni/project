Amortization Calculations

Amortization is when you pay off debt over a set amount of time in equal installments. Each month you will have a payment that will consist of the principal and the interest. The principal is the part that lowers the balance while the interest is the part that goes to the bank as interest on the loan. With an amortization, the principal starts out small and gradually gets bigger, vice versa, the interest starts big and gradually gets smaller. This is because at first, the balance is high, therefore more interest. As the balance gets lower, there is less interest and more principal. This website demonstrates how to calculate the monthly interest, principal and total cost of the loan.

Important Terms

  • Loan Amount: Total amount of money you are borrowing.
  • Loan Term: The time it will take to pay off the loan.
  • Interest Rate: The yearly rate also known as APR(Annual Percentage Rate).
  • Monthly Payment: Minimum payment set by lender.
  • Principal Amount: Amount going towards the loan.
  • Interest Amount: Amount going towards interest.
  • Balance: Amount left on loan after principal is paid.

Loan Example

Loan Amount: $10,000
Loan Term: 12 Months
Interest: 4.50%
Monthly Payment: $853.79

Steps to calculate monthly interest and principal

  1. First, we convert the percentage rate into a decimal and divide by 12 months.
    • (4.50/100) / (12) = 0.00375
  2. Then, we multiply that result with the loan amount.
    • 0.00375 * $10,000 = $37.50
  3. We now know the monthly interest. To get the principal, we subtract the interest from it.
    • $853.79 - $37.50 = $816.29
  4. Now we can subtract the principal from the loan amount and get our new loan balance.
    • $10,000 - $816.29 = $9,183.71
  5. We repeat the process from step 2 with the new loan balance and continue to do this until
    we have done the entire term. Then we can add up the interest from every month to get
    the total interest paid. This will give us the cost of the loan.
